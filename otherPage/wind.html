Short Course: Harnessing the Wind

As part of my continuous professional and academic development in sustainable energy and fluid mechanics, I attended a specialized short course titled "Harnessing the Wind: Analytical, Numerical and Experimental Approaches to Wind Energy" conducted by the Institute of Energy and Environmental Studies (IEES) at Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET).

This three-day intensive program provided a holistic understanding of wind energy systems through a blend of theoretical foundations, simulation training, and experimental techniques. The course was led by national and international experts in wind and renewable energy.

Course Highlights

Topics covered included aerodynamic principles of wind turbines, site assessment techniques, and performance evaluation metrics. Special emphasis was given to integrated modeling approaches using software like ANSYS and modeFrontier. I received practical training in CFD, Multi-Disciplinary Optimization (MDO), and simulation-driven turbine design.

One of the most valuable experiences was working on real-world case studies involving both onshore and offshore wind energy systems. Experimental modules introduced us to wind tunnel testing and result validation techniques for blade design and turbine aerodynamics.

The course also provided training in interpreting CFD results, optimizing turbine performance, and understanding the lifecycle of wind energy systems. An optional wind farm site visit was proposed to offer industrial context to the learning modules.

Outcome & Impact

I successfully completed the course and received a certificate of participation. The knowledge and skills acquired—from analytical methods to hands-on simulations—greatly enhanced my perspective on sustainable energy. This experience has equipped me with valuable tools applicable to both academic research and real-world renewable energy projects.
