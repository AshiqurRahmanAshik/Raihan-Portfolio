Undergraduate Thesis: Aerodynamic Optimization of MAV Wing Geometry

As part of my Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering at Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET), I undertook a one-year undergraduate research thesis titled "Effect of Swept Angle and Wing Profile on the Aerodynamic Performance of Fixed-Wing Micro Aerial Vehicle (MAV)". This research was supervised by Dr. Md. Rokunuzzaman, Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering, RUET.

The main objective was to study how the wing’s geometric characteristics—specifically sweep angle and airfoil shape—affect aerodynamic performance of MAVs at low Reynolds numbers. With MAVs increasingly used in reconnaissance, disaster response, and environmental monitoring, improving their lift-efficiency and maneuverability is critical.

Research Workflow

The study began with an extensive literature review on airfoil performance and low-speed vehicle aerodynamics. I then designed multiple wing types with different sweep angles (0°–30°), using the S5010 airfoil in various configurations: Baseline, Tapered, Backward Swept, Forward Swept, and combinations with tapered tips.

Using SolidWorks, I created 3D models of each wing while keeping fuselage geometry constant. For simulations, I used ANSYS Fluent with RANS (k-ω SST and IDDES) models to analyze performance indicators like lift coefficient, drag coefficient, pressure distribution, and flow separation behavior.

Simulation & Results

Mesh quality and independence were ensured using Y+ optimization, proper inflation layers, and minimal skewness. The results showed significant performance differences among wing types. Moderate backward-swept wings delayed stall and improved lift characteristics, while some profiles achieved higher lift-to-drag ratios at specific angles of attack.

Results were visualized using Tecplot and OriginLab with Cp distributions, pressure fields, and velocity vectors. The findings helped identify the most promising wing configuration for MAV applications, offering valuable insights into efficient and stable MAV flight design.

Skills & Impact

Through this thesis, I deepened my expertise in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), aerodynamic theory, and design optimization using engineering software. The thesis contributed to two international conferences and sparked my ongoing interest in biomimetic aerodynamics and UAV design.
