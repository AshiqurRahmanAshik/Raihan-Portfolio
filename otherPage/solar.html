Project Name: Hybrid Solar PV/T Desalination System Using Solar Still

Our team’s goal was to develop an innovative and sustainable solution that addresses two pressing global problems: energy efficiency and scarcity of clean water. We designed and experimentally assessed a hybrid solar PV/T desalination solution that combines solar electricity production with water purification using passive solar stills.

PV panels are often less efficient the hotter they become from sunlight. To address this heat challenge, we placed the solar still directly beneath the PV panel. This allowed the still to draw heat away from the PV along with sufficient energy to evaporate and condense water vapor, yielding distilled drinking water.

My Role in the Project

My role in this project consisted mostly of system design, simulation, and field implementation. I used SolidWorks and Autodesk Fusion 360 to model the structural layout and thermal contact between the PV panel and the still.

For computational simulation, I used ANSYS Fluent to observe heat transfer behavior including conduction, convection, temperature distributions, and flow patterns. I also conducted experimental tests in a real-world environment to monitor system performance, compare it to simulation data, and adjust design specifications.

We also utilized OriginLab for graphing and statistical analyses of experiments including temperature profiles, water generation rates, and PV panel efficiencies before and after cooling.

Project Outcome

The results showed promising outcomes. We witnessed a clear gain in electrical efficiency of the PV panel due to passive cooling, along with consistent clean water generation every day. This hybrid system proved to be highly energy-efficient and scalable—particularly suitable for off-grid, rural, or arid areas with limited access to electricity and clean water.

The PV/T system is entirely passive and requires no additional energy except sunlight—making it an energy-efficient, low-cost, and environmentally friendly solution.

Key Takeaways

This project allowed me to explore the intersection of mechanical design, thermal systems, and environmental engineering. It provided hands-on experience in creating practical solutions aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly in clean energy and clean water accessibility.

Overall, this work is a meaningful step toward decentralized and self-sufficient renewable energy systems.
